Cyber Security Challenge



Cyber Security Challenge - Ireland

Cyber Security Challenge Ireland, organised by Zero Days, is a national programme that aims to introduce students to the world of cyber security through a series of gamified cyber-security competitions and challenges, both in-person and online. Participants who show potential in these events will be invited to take part in our training bootcamps to further develop their skills and a chance to represent team Ireland at the European Cyber Security Challenge.

European Cyber Security Challenge

The European Cybersecurity Challenge (ECSC) is an annual event, powered by the EU Agency for Cybersecurity (ENISA), that brings together young cyber-security talent from across Europe to network, collaborate and compete. The ECSC aims to encourage young people to pursue a career in cybersecurity, by enhancing participants' abilities and connecting them with the industry and with their peers. Supported by the European Commission and EU Member States, in a broader sense the ECSC falls within the overall context of the EU's Cybersecurity Strategy and the NIS Directive.

Contestants solve security related challenges from domains such as:

  • Web and Network security
  • Mobile security
  • Cryptography
  • Reverse Engineering
  • Digital Forensics

Eligibility Criteria and Our Team

Each year Ireland sends a team of ten players to participate in the European Cyber Security Challenge. This year's finals take place in Hamar in Norway, from the 24th to 27th October. Ten players (Irish citizens or Irish passport holders), aged between 14-25 are chosen from the bootcamps and qualifying CTFs to travel to the ECSC host country.

Previous ECSC finals have taken place in:
  • Dusseldorf, Germany, 2016
  • London, UK, 2017
  • Malaga, Spain, 2018
  • Bucharest, Romania, 2019
  • Prague, Czech Republic, 2021
  • Vienna, Austria, 2022
  • Hamar, Norway, 2023
